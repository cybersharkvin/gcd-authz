% SUGGESTED TITLE: Constitutive Authorization at the Decoding Boundary: Grammar-Constrained Decoding as a Positive, Generation-Time Security Control for LLM Agents
\begin{abstract}
\label{sec:frontmatter}
We enforce tool-call authorization for large-language-model agents at the decoding boundary, applying complete mediation \cite{saltzer1975} to the token sampler: a per-request authorization grammar \(G_s\) makes out-of-scope tokens ungenerable at the sampler \cite{willard2023,dong2024xgrammar} before any token is committed. Our thesis is constitutive vs.\ corrective enforcement. A grammar-constrained decoder (\texttt{c}) and a post-parse allowlist gate (\texttt{d}) enforce the same policy over \(L(G_s)\) but differ in completeness; the empirical signature is emission, the count of times the unauthorized artifact is produced --- \(0\) under \texttt{c} by construction, \(>0\) under \texttt{d}. Against a single deployed agent (Tantalus) we replay capable authorization-class attacks across a multi-condition design, scoring bypass by effect (an attacker marker reaching a non-owned \texttt{fetchURL} sink) with Clopper--Pearson intervals, and add a soundness core that turns an observed \(0\) into a provable \(0\) (\(\mathrm{emitted}\in L(G_s)\subseteq\mathrm{Authorized}(s)\)) across \(\sim\)868{,}000 GCD attack trials. Across five datasets (\(\sim\)2{,}460{,}677 trials over three pretraining lineages): the capable Mistral-Small-4-119B anchor still leaks under the full behavioral stack \texttt{a4} (bypass 1.82\% [1.43, 2.29], CI-lower \(>0\)) while \texttt{c} is 0.00\% [0, 0.09]; the Gemma-4-31B lineage leaks under \texttt{a4} (33.80\% [31.73, 35.92]) while \texttt{c} is 0.00\% [0, 0.18]; and an abliterated steelman \cite{arditi2024} moves 97.85\% \(\to\) 0\%. Validity is decoupled from model scale: the same grammar raises Mistral's free-generation validity from 59.2\% to 99.8\%. Honesty envelope: the headline is carried by the two capable anchors, not the weak 1.7B workhorse; external validity spans 3 lineages with Gemma a single-anchor caveat; the registered 2\,pp non-inferiority TOST, run powered (observed \(-1.85\)\,pp, cluster-bootstrap 95\% CI \([-7.07, +3.43]\), half-width \(\approx 5.3\)\,pp), does not establish non-inferiority; cold grammar-compile exceeds the registered 25\,ms\,p99 target (\(\approx\)59--61\,ms\,p99, though amortized per-trial \(\approx\)0.003\,ms); and \texttt{c\_sealed}'s 0\% is partly true by construction.
\end{abstract}
