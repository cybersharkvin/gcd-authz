% SUGGESTED TITLE: Constitutive Authorization at the Decoding Boundary: Grammar-Constrained Decoding as a Positive, Generation-Time Security Control for LLM Agents
\begin{abstract}
\label{sec:frontmatter}
Tool-call authorization for large-language-model agents is enforced at the decoding boundary, applying complete mediation \cite{saltzer1975} to the token sampler: a per-request authorization grammar \(G_s\) makes out-of-scope tokens ungenerable at the sampler \cite{willard2023,dong2024xgrammar} before any token is committed. The thesis is constitutive versus corrective enforcement. A grammar-constrained decoder (\texttt{c}) and a post-parse allowlist gate (\texttt{d}) enforce the same policy over \(L(G_s)\) but differ in completeness. The empirical signature is emission, the count of times the unauthorized artifact is produced: \(0\) under \texttt{c} by construction, \(>0\) under \texttt{d}. Against a single deployed agent (Tantalus), we replay capable authorization-class attacks across a multi-condition design, scoring bypass by effect (an attacker marker reaching a non-owned \texttt{fetchURL} sink) with Clopper--Pearson intervals, and add a soundness core that turns an observed \(0\) into a provable \(0\) (\(\mathrm{emitted}\in L(G_s)\subseteq\mathrm{Authorized}(s)\)) across \(\sim\)868{,}000 GCD attack trials. The evaluation spans five datasets and three pretraining lineages, totaling \(\sim\)6.1\,million individual inference calls and over half a billion (\(\sim\)544\,million) generated tokens. The capable Mistral-Small-4-119B anchor still leaks under the full behavioral stack \texttt{a4} (bypass 1.82\% [1.43, 2.29], CI-lower \(>0\)) while \texttt{c} is 0.00\% [0, 0.09]; the Gemma-4-31B lineage leaks under \texttt{a4} (33.80\% [31.73, 35.92]) while \texttt{c} is 0.00\% [0, 0.18]; and an abliterated steelman \cite{arditi2024} moves 97.85\% \(\to\) 0\%. Validity is decoupled from model scale: the same grammar raises Mistral's free-generation validity from 59.2\% to 99.8\%. However, the guarantee is scoped to action integrity over enumerable-action sinks (the \texttt{fetchURL}, channel, path, and recipient parameters), not the confidentiality of free-text content, and the full set of limitations is detailed in the paper.
\end{abstract}
